# PresentLM Prototype

<p style={{ lineHeight: '1.6em' }}>
  PresentLM is a prototype that explores how generative AI can support spoken presentation and explanation through synchronized narration, slide progression, and interactive questioning.  
  The system treats slides as temporal reference points and focuses on <strong>what is said</strong>, <strong>when it is said</strong>, and <strong>how it is explained</strong>.
</p>

---

## Core Problems

<div style={{ display: 'flex', flexWrap: 'wrap', justifyContent: 'center', gap: '1em' }}>
  <div style={{ padding: '1em', borderRadius: '8px', flex: '1 1 250px', maxWidth: '300px' }}>
    <strong>Presentations lose meaning when speech is missing</strong>  
    <em>Slides alone do not convey reasoning, emphasis, or narrative structure.</em>
  </div>

  <div style={{ padding: '1em', borderRadius: '8px', flex: '1 1 250px', maxWidth: '300px' }}>
    <strong>Rehearsal lacks interaction and feedback</strong>  
    <em>Practicing alone offers no sense of timing, flow, or alternative explanations.</em>
  </div>

  <div style={{ padding: '1em', borderRadius: '8px', flex: '1 1 250px', maxWidth: '300px' }}>
    <strong>Learning from slides is passive</strong>  
    <em>Students cannot ask questions or request clarification when reviewing content independently.</em>
  </div>
</div>

## Core Possibilities

<div style={{ display: 'flex', flexWrap: 'wrap', justifyContent: 'center', gap: '1em' }}>
  <div style={{ padding: '1em', borderRadius: '8px', flex: '1 1 250px', maxWidth: '300px' }}>
    <strong>Explainable Presentations</strong>  
    <em>Slides are accompanied by structured spoken narration, making explanations explicit rather than implicit.</em>
  </div>

  <div style={{ padding: '1em', borderRadius: '8px', flex: '1 1 250px', maxWidth: '300px' }}>
    <strong>Realistic Rehearsal</strong>  
    <em>Presenters can experience the flow, pacing, and transitions of a presentation without a live audience.</em>
  </div>

  <div style={{ padding: '1em', borderRadius: '8px', flex: '1 1 250px', maxWidth: '300px' }}>
    <strong>Active Learning</strong>  
    <em>Learners can pause, replay, and ask questions at any moment, transforming passive slide decks into interactive learning experiences.</em>
  </div>
</div>
